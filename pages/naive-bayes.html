Overview

This page provides details on the Naive Bayes model used to analyze NBA data.

Graphs and Analysis

Explore detailed graphs and metrics from the Naive Bayes model:

    The Naive Bayes model is a simple probabilistic classifier based on applying Bayes' theorem with strong (naive) independence assumptions between the features. The model has an accuracy of 60.2% and a cross-validation score of 60.1%. The confusion matrix shows that the model tends to predict home team victories at a very high rate (Predicted label 1). This leads to a lot of false positives, which is something to consider when comparing future models.
